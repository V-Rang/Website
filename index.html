My name is Venugopal Ranganathan and I graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from BITS Pilani in 2021. Since then, I have been working as a project associate in the Nanoscale Energy Transport Lab (NETLab) at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc).


I am passionate about applying computational and simulation sciences in tackling problems in the domain of fluid-thermal engineering. During my undergraduate, I have undertaken projects in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), microfluidics, refrigeration & air-conditioning, and computational data science.


My present work at IISc is on formulating finite element solutions of the phonon Boltzmann Transport Equation to estimate and modify the thermal properties of solid crystals.

Modal Decomposition of Fluid Flows and its Application to Compressor Acoustic Characteristics

Bachelor's Thesis

Mercedes-Benz Research &

Development India

February 2021 - June 2021

An increase in the computational powers of present-day computers has made it possible to model and simulate increasingly complex flows. Large data produced from these simulations has increased the burden on storage systems and made it challenging to draw meaningful inferences.

Proper Orthogonal Decomposition and Dynamic Mode Decomposition are applied to CFD pressure data of an automotive compressor to make insights about the locations and frequencies of noise produced.
